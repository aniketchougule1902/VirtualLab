References & Resources

Academic references, textbooks, and online resources for linear algebra

Textbooks on Linear Algebra

  • 1
    Strang, G. (2016). Introduction to Linear Algebra (5th ed.). Wellesley-Cambridge Press.

    A classic textbook with clear explanations and practical applications. Excellent for beginners.

  • 2
    Lay, D. C., Lay, S. R., & McDonald, J. J. (2021). Linear Algebra and Its Applications (6th ed.). Pearson.

    Comprehensive coverage with applications to computer science, engineering, and economics.

  • 3
    Axler, S. (2015). Linear Algebra Done Right (3rd ed.). Springer.

    Theoretical approach emphasizing understanding over computation. Recommended for mathematics majors.

  • 4
    Poole, D. (2014). Linear Algebra: A Modern Introduction (4th ed.). Cengage Learning.

    Modern approach with early emphasis on vectors and geometry. Includes technology projects.

  • 5
    Anton, H., & Rorres, C. (2014). Elementary Linear Algebra (11th ed.). Wiley.

    Classic textbook with comprehensive coverage and numerous exercises.

Online Learning Resources

  • 6
    Khan Academy - Linear Algebra

    https://www.khanacademy.org/math/linear-algebra

    Free comprehensive video tutorials and practice exercises covering all linear algebra topics.

  • 7
    MIT OpenCourseWare - Linear Algebra

    https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/

    Complete course materials from MIT, including video lectures by Gilbert Strang.

  • 8
    3Blue1Brown - Essence of Linear Algebra

    https://www.3blue1brown.com/topics/linear-algebra

  • 9
    Paul's Online Math Notes - Linear Algebra

    Detailed notes and examples covering undergraduate linear algebra topics.

  • 10
    Wolfram MathWorld - Linear Algebra

    https://mathworld.wolfram.com/topics/LinearAlgebra.html

    Technical reference for linear algebra concepts, definitions, and formulas.

Academic Papers and Articles

  • 15
    Carlson, D. (1993). Teaching Linear Algebra: Must the Fog Always Roll In? College Mathematics Journal, 24(1), 29-40.

    Discussion of pedagogical challenges in teaching linear algebra and strategies to address them.

  • 16
    Dorier, J. L. (Ed.). (2000). On the Teaching of Linear Algebra. Springer.

    Collection of papers on the didactics of linear algebra from an international perspective.

  • 17
    Harel, G. (2000). Three Principles of Learning and Teaching Mathematics. Journal of Mathematical Behavior, 19(1), 79-91.

    Principles applied to linear algebra education with emphasis on conceptual understanding.
